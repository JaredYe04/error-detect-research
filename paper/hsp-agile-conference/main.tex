\begin{abstract}
Ordered guard specifications enforce first-match precedence; large language
models (LLMs) and test-feedback repair miss thin overlap regions that formal
contracts pin down.
We present \textsc{SgDP}, instantiated as HSP-Agile for SOFL/FSF, with three
mechanisms: SpecIR first-match regions, a typed Semantic Feedback IR for
repair, and a conjunctive release gate.
\textbf{Lead claim (C2):} under equal repair budget $K{=}3$, full Semantic
Feedback IR improves mean formal conformance by \textbf{+7.7\,pp} vs.\
unstructured test-only (E6, $n{=}120$; paired 14/4/102; bootstrap 95\% CI
$[2.3,\,13.6]$\,pp; Wilcoxon $p{=}0.018$)---scoped to test-only, not every
structured trace (E14).
\textbf{Prevention (C3):} conjunctive Accept yields E2 PDR/FAR 95.0\%/5.0\%
vs.\ B2 91.2\%/8.8\% (bootstrap CI excludes~0).
\textbf{Deploy B2 by default (C4)} unless $\mathrm{Accept}{=}1$ or
FAR${\le}5\%$ is required.
Equal-$K$ \texttt{M\_eq} reaches ${+}2.5$\,pp vs.\ B2; industrial-pattern
($n{=}31$) and published-industrial ($n{=}10$) pilots support B2 as the
mean-Conf.\ default.
Vendor \texttt{.asfl} import is supported when SpecTool/NDA packs land under
\texttt{vendor/}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
%======================================================================
Formal specifications buy precision that informal prose cannot match, but that
precision becomes a liability when LLMs write the implementation: the model has
no native notion of guard order, so it produces code that is statistically
plausible yet formally wrong on thin overlap slices~\cite{chen2021codex,dakhel2023copilot}.
SOFL's Functional Specification Format (FSF) is an ordered list of scenarios
under first-match precedence~\cite{liu1998sofl,liu2004soflbook,li2022qrs_companion}.
Unit tests almost never sample the region
$G_i\wedge\bigwedge_{j<i}\neg G_j$, so test-based repair leaves ordering
defects in place~\cite{zhu1997specification,ammann2016testing}.
The practical stakes are high in signalling and finance: a route-lock that
ignores conflict, or a fraud-hold that loses to a transfer path, is a
conformance failure even when most random tests pass.

\runin{Motivating example.}
Consider an industrial monitoring alert: negative sensor levels must raise
\texttt{Error} before any threshold-based \texttt{Critical} path can fire.
On input $(\mathit{level},\mathit{threshold})=(-3,-10)$, both $G_1$
(\texttt{level < 0}) and $G_2$ (\texttt{level >= threshold}) hold; FSF
mandates \texttt{Error} via~$S_1$.
A typical LLM candidate evaluates the threshold comparison first and returns
\texttt{Critical}---invisible to suites that draw non-negative thresholds.
The same pattern appears in published railway SOFL cases: fault and conflict
guards must preempt route-lock or gate-motion commands~\cite{liu1998railwaycrossing,luo2017railway}.
Our pipeline turns that failure into a typed repair record naming the violated
scenario, then re-synthesises until witnesses agree or the budget is exhausted.

\runin{Research question.}
Can specification-conformance defects in LLM-generated code be prevented by
using the specification itself as the primary evidence source, without full
deductive verification?
Our answer is \emph{conditional}: yes when release requires conjunctive Accept
or prevention metrics; otherwise prefer lightweight test-feedback.
We therefore separate \emph{mechanism} claims (typed feedback, Accept gate)
from a \emph{deployment} claim (when to pay for them).
This separation is the difference between a tool paper that always recommends
the heaviest loop and a deployment-aware method that matches cost to the
release bar.

\runin{Threats this paper confronts up front.}
(1)~Synthetic hard FSF (BENCH-120) is overlap-filtered---we bound transfer with
E10, industrial-pattern, and published-industrial pilots.
(2)~Strengthened E1 uses $K{=}5$ and a multi-factor bundle---we isolate equal-$K$
Conf.\ via \texttt{M\_eq} and lead Conf.\ mechanism via E6.
(3)~No public Casco/Mitsubishi \texttt{.asfl} dumps exist; we reconstruct
published industrial decision tables and provide a vendor import path for
SpecTool/NDA packs.

\runin{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{C1---SpecIR:} notation-agnostic first-match regions
    (engineering hinge; overlap tertiles on E3/E10 are
    \emph{non-monotone}---not a ranking signal alone).
  \item \textbf{C2---Typed Semantic Feedback IR:} ${+}7.7$\,pp vs.\
    unstructured test-only under equal $K{=}3$ (E6; CI excludes~0; E14
    scopes uniqueness).
  \item \textbf{C3---Conjunctive Accept:} full witness conformance
    $\wedge$ clear high-severity pattern screen (E2 PDR/FAR).
  \item \textbf{C4---Deployment boundary:} prefer B2 by default; escalate
    to~M iff $\mathrm{Accept}{=}1$ or target FAR${\le}5\%$; pilots reinforce
    the default.
\end{itemize}

Section~II positions related systems; Section~III details SpecIR, Feedback IR,
and Accept; Section~IV fixes corpora and protocol hygiene; Section~V reports

A companion long report carries full ablations (E14--E17), B3--B6 baselines,

\section{Related Work}
%======================================================================
Closest LLM+verifier systems differ on whether witnesses respect first-match
order, whether repair carries scenario structure, and whether prevention
(PDR/FAR) is measured (Table~\ref{tab:rw}).

\runin{Proof-oriented oracles.}
DafnyBench and Clover target proof-oriented
oracles~\cite{loughman2024dafnybench,sun2024clover}; success is theorem
discharge, not first-match region coverage.
Our Conf.\ metric is witness agreement under SpecIR, not a proof certificate.
CEGIS-style loops with SMT counterexamples are closer in spirit, but typically
target synthesis of loop invariants or programs from logical specs rather than
ordered-guard FSF with typed repair records.

\runin{Verbal and agentic repair.}
Self-Refine, Self-Debug, and Reflexion raise generation success via critique or
verbal memory~\cite{madaan2023selfrefine,chen2023selfdebug,shinn2023reflexion}
but do not emit region-typed failure records.
CodeT and coding agents optimise repository tasks without an FSF
oracle~\cite{chen2022codet,wang2024opendevin,yang2024sweagent}.
LLM-based APR patches failing tests~\cite{xia2023chatrepair,jiang2023impact};
we evaluate unstructured test feedback as B2 and isolate typed IR in E6.
Break-It-Fix-It emphasises natural-language repair
datasets~\cite{yasunaga2021breakitfix}; our residual defects are ordering /
overlap, not syntax.

\runin{Adjacent notations.}
Alloy and TLA+ encode structured
constraints~\cite{jackson2002alloy,lamport2002tla}; SpecIR adapters are the
intended bridge when guards are first-match and SMT-encodable.
SOFL itself has a long industrial and teaching
lineage~\cite{liu1998sofl,liu2004soflbook,li2022qrs_companion}; we reuse that
contract shape rather than inventing a new specification language.

\runin{Industrial SOFL.}
Railway interlocking at Casco Signal~\cite{luo2017railway} and crossing
controllers with Mitsubishi~\cite{liu1998railwaycrossing} motivate ordered-guard
contracts but do not release public \texttt{.asfl} dumps.
Teaching cases (ATM, hotel, banking) appear in Liu's SOFL
book~\cite{liu2004soflbook}; Agile-SOFL SpecTool demos are available only via
author collaboration~\cite{liu1998sofl}.
Related SOFL fault-prevention work studies specification-to-program
transformation defects~\cite{li2023iet_faultprevention,li2022qrs_companion};
we target LLM repair under an executable SpecIR oracle instead.
Broader formal-methods surveys and mutation testing
background~\cite{woodcock2009formalsurvey,demillo1978mutation,leino2010dafny}
frame why witness-based Conf.\ is a pragmatic middle ground between unit tests
and full proofs.
\textsc{SgDP} combines first-match SMT witnesses, typed Semantic Feedback IR,
and conjunctive Accept under PDR/FAR.
We evaluate verbal/trace loops as B3--B5 and verifier-loop FSF as B6 in the
long report; the conference cut focuses on B1/B2/M and E6/E2.

\section{Approach}
%======================================================================
\subsection{SpecIR and first-match witnesses}
For task $t$ with ordered scenarios $\langle s_1,\ldots,s_n\rangle$, SpecIR
exposes first-match regions as a shared hinge across notations (SOFL/FSF,
Mini-Z, Mini-StateMachine adapters).
Each scenario $s_i=(G_i,P_i)$ pairs a guard with a postcondition; FSF selects
the least $i$ with $G_i(\mathbf{x})$ and requires $P_i(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y})$.
For scenario $s_i$, the witness generator solves
\begin{equation}
  \Phi_i \;\triangleq\; \hat{g}_i(\mathbf{x})
  \;\land\; \bigwedge_{j<i}\lnot\hat{g}_j(\mathbf{x})
\end{equation}
with Z3~\cite{demoura2008z3,barrett2018smt}, yielding suite $\mathcal{D}(t)$.
Without the negated higher guards, a model of $g_i$ alone may still belong to
$s_j$ for $j{<}i$---the exact failure mode of the motivating example.
An \emph{others} witness uses $\bigwedge_i\lnot\hat{g}_i$ when nonempty; we
disclose a prior others-witness bug (others encoded without $\neg$ prior
guards) as a measurement correction that previously inflated false failures.
Soundness is conditional on a faithful SMT encoding of SpecIR guards
(integer/boolean fragments in our corpora).
Random tests undersample $\Phi_i$ as overlap grows, so ordering bugs survive
green CI~\cite{zhu1997specification,ammann2016testing}; SpecIR makes those
slices first-class for Conf.\ and repair.

\subsection{Worked repair step}
On $(-3,-10)$, the buggy candidate returns \texttt{Critical} where the oracle
requires \texttt{Error}.
The Semantic Feedback IR records (abbreviated):
\begin{lstlisting}
{"violation_type":"ordering","scenario_index":1,
 "constraint_text":"level < 0",
 "inputs":{"level":-3,"threshold":-10},
 "expected":"Error","observed":"Critical",
 "suggested_fix":"test level < 0 first"}
\end{lstlisting}
\texttt{FeedbackRenderer} projects this JSON into the repair prompt; E6
isolates \texttt{test\_only} / \texttt{test\_expected} / \texttt{semantic\_ir}
surfaces under identical~$K$.

\subsection{Semantic Feedback IR and Accept}
The typed IR carries at least: \texttt{violation\_type} (ordering, postcondition,
others), \texttt{scenario\_index}, \texttt{constraint\_text}, concrete
\texttt{inputs}, \texttt{expected}/\texttt{observed} outputs, and an optional
\texttt{suggested\_fix} hint.
Conformance and release:
\begin{align}
  \mathrm{Conf}(c,t)
  &= \tfrac{1}{|\mathcal{D}(t)|}
     \sum_{\mathbf{x}\in\mathcal{D}(t)}
     \mathbf{1}[f_c(\mathbf{x})=g_t(\mathbf{x})], \\
  \mathrm{Accept}(c,t)
  &\iff \mathrm{Conf}(c,t)=1 \,\land\, P^H(c)=0.
\end{align}
Mode~B2 uses unstructured test pass/fail only---no scenario index, no
constraint text.
Controlled E6 isolates feedback content under full~M at \textbf{equal} $K{=}3$
(lead Conf.\ claim): variant~A is test-only; B adds expected outputs; C is the
full Semantic IR.
Equal-$K$ mode \texttt{M\_eq} ($K{=}3$, \texttt{semantic\_ir}, advisory gate)
is the Conf.\ ranking hygiene mode vs.\ B2.
Strengthened E1 ($K{=}5$, \texttt{execution\_trace\_matched}, advisory gate)
is a multi-factor \emph{bundle}---stress-test only, not single-factor C2.
E14 further shows that length-matched execution traces can outperform
\texttt{semantic\_ir} on mean Conf.; uniqueness is therefore scoped to
unstructured test feedback, not to ``any structured string.''

\subsection{Pipeline}
Algorithm~\ref{alg:sgdp} summarises one task.
Synthesise from FSF text $\to$ check on $\mathcal{D}(t)$ $\to$
if not Accept, render Feedback IR and repair, up to budget~$K$; on exhaustion
return $\arg\max_k\mathrm{Conf}(c_k,t)$ (best-effort).
Prevention evaluation injects specification-confusion and
implementation-screening mutants and scores PDR/FAR on the faulty set.
High-severity pattern screen $P^H$ blocks known dangerous templates
(e.g., inverted priority, missing fault preemption) even when mean Conf.\ is
high.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{\textsc{SgDP} repair loop (HSP-Agile instantiation)}
\label{alg:sgdp}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require FSF task $t$, budget $K$, feedback mode $m$
\State $\mathcal{D}(t)\gets$ SpecIR witnesses via Z3
\State $c^\star\gets\bot$; $\mathit{best}\gets 0$
\For{$k=1$ to $K$}
  \State $c\gets$ synthesise / repair($t$, feedback$_{k-1}$)
  \State $\mathit{conf}\gets\mathrm{Conf}(c,t)$ on $\mathcal{D}(t)$
  \If{$\mathit{conf}>\mathit{best}$} $c^\star\gets c$; $\mathit{best}\gets\mathit{conf}$
  \EndIf
  \If{$\mathrm{Accept}(c,t)$} \Return $c$
  \EndIf
  \State feedback$_k\gets$ render($m$, failures on $\mathcal{D}(t)$)
\EndFor
\State \Return $c^\star$ \Comment{best-effort argmax Conf.}
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\runin{Design rationale.}
We deliberately do \emph{not} claim that SpecIR alone yields monotone Conf.\
gains with overlap density: E3/E10 tertiles are non-monotone, so overlap is an
engineering hinge for witness generation, not a deployment trigger.
The deployable levers are feedback typing (E6) and the Accept/prevention gate
(E2).
Adapters (Mini-Z, Mini-StateMachine) share SpecIR; FSF lowering is engineering
for the SOFL instantiation, not a claimed contribution.
The same loop applies when a partner later supplies \texttt{.asfl}: only the
SpecIR front-end changes; Conf., Feedback IR, and Accept stay fixed---so vendor
pilots test transfer, not a new method.

\section{Experimental Setup}
%======================================================================
\runin{Benchmarks.}
\textbf{BENCH-120:} synthetic hard FSF (5 inputs, 8 scenarios) retained by a Z3
overlap filter; primary ranking corpus for E1/E6/E12.
\textbf{E10:} removes the filter ($n{=}100$) to bound filter bias.
\textbf{Industrial-pattern} ($n{=}31$): practitioner-style decision tables
(banking, telecom, SCADA, \ldots)---not proprietary dumps.
\textbf{Published-industrial pilot} ($n{=}10$): FSF \emph{reconstructed} from
published railway crossing / interlocking and ATM/banking SOFL
cases~\cite{liu1998railwaycrossing,luo2017railway,liu2004soflbook}
(\texttt{benchmarks/published\_industrial\_pilot.json}).
Vendor \texttt{.asfl} files, when placed under
\texttt{vendor/agile-sofl-toolchain/examples}, import via
\texttt{scripts/import\_vendor\_asfl.py} (Casco/Mitsubishi dumps require NDA
and are not redistributed).

\runin{Modes.}
B1 one-shot; B2 test-feedback ($K{=}3$); M (strengthened bundle, $K{=}5$);
\texttt{M\_eq} (equal-$K$ hygiene, $K{=}3$, \texttt{semantic\_ir}, advisory).
E6 variants A/B/C hold full~M fixed and vary only feedback surface.

\runin{Metrics.}
Mean Conf.; Strict / $\mathrm{Accept}$; PDR/FAR on mutants; latency and LLM
calls; paired wins/losses/ties with bootstrap CIs where claimed.
Primary LLM: \texttt{ecnu-plus} (Qwen-family); secondary campus and commercial
endpoints bound model dependence.
Repair temperature $T_\text{repair}{=}0$.
Witness budget: up to 32 formal cases per task in strengthened runs
(\texttt{formal\_max\_cases=32}); E6 uses the same witness suite across A/B/C.

\runin{Mutants (E2).}
Implementation-screening mutants flip guard order, drop fault/conflict
preemption, or weaken postconditions while keeping surface syntax plausible
($n{=}852$ screened set; pooled with specification-confusion yields $n{=}1704$).
PDR is the fraction of faulty candidates rejected by the gate; FAR is the
false-alarm rate on the same faulty pool under the release predicate.

\runin{Protocol hygiene.}
Cite E6 for C2; \texttt{M\_eq} for equal-$K$ Conf.\ ranking; strengthened E1
only as stress-test; never mix corpora in one claim sentence.
Authoritative run ids: \texttt{run\_feedback\_v2} (E6),
\texttt{prevention\_full\_v1} (E2), \texttt{run\_e1\_equal\_k\_v1},
\texttt{run\_e1\_m\_win\_v2}, \texttt{run\_pubind\_pilot\_v1}.

\subsection{RQ1: Typed Semantic Feedback IR (E6 / C2)}
Figure~\ref{fig:e6} and Table~\ref{tab:e6} hold $K$ and tasks fixed under
full~M ($K{=}3$, $n{=}120$).
Variant~C (full Semantic IR) reaches 86.9\% mean Conf.---\textbf{+7.7\,pp}
over test-only (A, 79.1\%) and ${+}8.0$\,pp over test+expected (B, 78.9\%).
Paired per-task: wins/losses/ties \textbf{14/4/102}; bootstrap 95\% CI
$[2.3,\,13.6]$\,pp; Wilcoxon $p{=}0.018$.
Most tasks tie because they already sit near ceiling under full~M; the 14 wins
concentrate on ordering-sensitive items where scenario index and constraint
text change the repair trajectory.
E14 length-matched sweep: \texttt{semantic\_ir} 75.1\% vs.\
\texttt{execution\_trace\_matched} 85.4\% (paired 5/19/96)---so C2 is
\emph{relative to unstructured test feedback}, not uniqueness among all
structured traces.
Hard combo seeds on \texttt{gemini-2.5-flash} ($n{=}40$ tasks $\times$ 3
seed types; \texttt{run\_ir\_combo\_seed\_gemini\_n40\_v1}) further support
C2: pooled FULL vs.\ \texttt{test\_only} ${+}33.2$\,pp and vs.\
\texttt{test\_expected} ${+}32.6$\,pp (both CI exclude~0); FULL vs.\
\texttt{ir\_no\_expected} does not---consistent with E14.
Practitioners who already emit rich execution traces may prefer those surfaces;
our claim is that unstructured pass/fail is the wrong default when SpecIR
witnesses are available.

\subsection{RQ2: Equal-$K$ ranking and E1 stress-test}
Equal-$K$ Conf.\ ranking (\texttt{run\_e1\_equal\_k\_v1}): \texttt{M\_eq}
100.0\% vs.\ B2 97.5\% (${+}2.5$\,pp; 3/120 wins, 0 losses).

\subsection{RQ3: Prevention (E2 / C3) and pattern gate}
On implementation-screening mutants ($n{=}852$), M reaches PDR 95.0\% / FAR
5.0\% vs.\ B2 91.2\% / 8.8\%; paired bootstrap 95\% CIs on the gaps exclude
zero (Fig.~\ref{fig:prev}).
The absolute PDR lift is modest (${+}3.8$\,pp) but operationally meaningful at
the FAR${\le}5\%$ bar that many safety-adjacent release processes target.
Pattern-guard value is measured here (and advisory E17), not by mean-Conf.\
ablation under an advisory gate (fixed-oracle A2 $\Delta$Conf${=}0$).
Fixed-oracle ablations vs.\ strengthened M: removing repair $-$25.8\,pp;
removing formal checker $-$17.5\,pp; removing pattern guard $0.0$\,pp on Conf.\
(measure gate via E2).
In other words: repair and witnesses move Conf.; the pattern screen moves
prevention---do not expect Conf.\ ablations to ``prove'' the gate.

\subsection{RQ4: Deployment boundary (C4)}
Canonical E3/E10 tertiles are \emph{non-monotone}---do not escalate on overlap
alone (Fig.~\ref{fig:overlap}).
\textbf{Decision rule:} prefer B2 by default; escalate to~M iff release
requires $\mathrm{Accept}{=}1$ \emph{or} target FAR${\le}5\%$
(Table~\ref{tab:deploy}).
On fixed-oracle E1, mean LLM calls for B2 ($\sim$1.18) and strengthened M
($\sim$1.16) are comparable; historical pre-fix Pareto exports showed larger
latency gaps when repair loops ran longer---treat latency as
configuration-dependent, not as a fixed $3\times$ tax.
Fig.~\ref{fig:latency} and Fig.~\ref{fig:pareto} illustrate the quality--cost
trade space used to motivate the default-B2 rule.

\section{Discussion and Threats}
%======================================================================
\runin{Practice.}
Match feedback depth to the release bar: B2 for mean Conf.\ / latency; M when
conjunctive Accept or FAR${\le}5\%$ is required.
Published-industrial and industrial-pattern pilots reinforce that default.
For CI/CD pre-merge gates, equal-$K$ \texttt{M\_eq} and strengthened~M have
comparable mean LLM calls to B2 on fixed-oracle E1; interactive IDE use may
still prefer B2.
Railway-style fault/conflict preemption is exactly where Accept and PDR/FAR
matter more than mean Conf.: a candidate that scores high mean Conf.\ but
misses a fault-first scenario must not ship.
A concrete playbook: (i)~run B2 in the inner loop; (ii)~nightly or pre-release
run M with Accept; (iii)~track FAR on a frozen mutant pack; (iv)~escalate the
inner loop only if FAR drifts above target or Accept failures cluster on
ordering classes.

\runin{Published pilot vs.\ vendor-\texttt{.asfl}.}
The $n{=}10$ published-industrial pilot is intentionally honest: guards are
reconstructed from papers and textbooks, not extracted from Casco production
databases.
That is enough to stress first-match preemption on railway/ATM/banking shapes
and to support C4's default-B2 recommendation on mean Conf.
It is \emph{not} a substitute for a partner NDA dump.
When SpecTool or NDA \texttt{.asfl} files arrive under
\texttt{vendor/agile-sofl-toolchain/examples}, re-export with
\texttt{import\_vendor\_asfl.py} and report a separate vendor-asfl table---same
metrics, clearer provenance.

\runin{What not to claim.}
Do not claim (i)~M dominates every seed (E12), (ii)~overlap tertiles predict
when to escalate (E3/E10), (iii)~Semantic IR is unique among all structured
traces (E14), or (iv)~published reconstructions are Casco production dumps.
Lead story remains E6 mechanism + E2 prevention + C4 deployment.

\runin{Threats to validity.}
(1)~\emph{Construct:} finite witnesses approximate Conf.; others-witness bug
disclosed as measurement correction that previously inflated false failures
($\sim$5\% Strict on historical E1).
(2)~\emph{Internal:} LLM non-determinism mitigated by $T_\text{repair}{=}0$ and
paired tests; equal-$K$ hygiene via \texttt{M\_eq}; E1 bundle is not E6.
(3)~\emph{External:} BENCH-120 is synthetic/overlap-filtered; E10, E11,
industrial-pattern, and published-industrial pilots bound transfer.
Reconstructions are not proprietary dumps.
Vendor \texttt{.asfl} pilots remain future work until SpecTool/NDA packs are
obtained via the Hosei contact path or partner NDA.
(4)~\emph{Conclusion:} do not read the $K{=}5$ E1 bundle as equal-$K$ C2;
power for tiny Conf.\ gaps is low, hence paired win rates and mechanism CIs.
(5)~\emph{Model:} ceiling effects on stronger models shrink E6 deltas---report
headroom explicitly when transferring claims.

\runin{Ethics and data.}
Industrial NDA artefacts must not be redistributed; published reconstructions
cite bibliographic provenance only.
Contact templates for SpecTool packs live in the artefact repository under
\texttt{vendor/REQUEST\_TEMPLATE.md} (Hosei starting point:
\texttt{sliu@hosei.ac.jp}).
Aggregate Conf./PDR only when partners forbid process-name disclosure.
Reproducibility artefacts (run ids, benchmark JSON, import scripts) accompany
the long report; this cut cites the same authoritative numbers card.

\runin{Reproducibility.}
Benchmarks, run ids, and summarisation scripts ship with the artefact package:
\texttt{benchmarks/published\_industrial\_pilot.json},
\texttt{scripts/import\_vendor\_asfl.py},
\texttt{paper/hsp-agile/scripts/e6\_paired\_analysis.py}, and
\texttt{AUTHORITATIVE\_NUMBERS.md}.
Primary claims must cite those run directories rather than regenerated ad-hoc
aggregates.
A long-report companion expands B3--B6, E14--E17, and full figure packs for
readers who need the complete experimental appendix.

\section{Conclusion}
%======================================================================
\textsc{SgDP}/HSP-Agile prevents first-match ordering defects with SpecIR
witnesses, typed Semantic Feedback IR, and conjunctive Accept.
Remember: (1)~E6 +7.7\,pp vs.\ test-only at $K{=}3$ (CI excludes~0; E14 scopes
uniqueness); (2)~E2 PDR/FAR justifies the gate; (3)~deploy B2 by default;
escalate for Accept or FAR${\le}5\%$; (4)~equal-$K$, pilots, and ceiling
stress-tests are secondary to the mechanism+deployment story.
On published-industrial and industrial-pattern corpora, mean Conf.\ already
favours B2---use M when the release bar demands Accept or prevention, not by
default.
Future work: NDA vendor \texttt{.asfl} pilots at scale, adaptive~$K$, and
SpecIR adapters beyond SOFL/FSF.
