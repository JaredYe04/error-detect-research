\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

Ordered guard specifications say which scenario must fire first when several
guards hold.
Large language models rarely obey that rule on thin overlap regions that
formal contracts pin down; unit tests almost never sample those regions, so
test-based repair leaves ordering defects in place.

We present \textbf{deployment-aware specification-guided repair} via the
\textsc{SgDP} framework, instantiated as \textbf{HSP-Agile} for SOFL/FSF.
Three mechanisms address that gap: \emph{SpecIR} semantic regions (first-match
partitions), a typed \emph{Semantic Feedback IR} for repair, and a
\emph{conjunctive} release gate (full witness conformance $\wedge$ no
high-severity structural pattern).

\textbf{Lead empirical result:} under identical repair budget $K{=}3$, full
Semantic Feedback IR improves mean formal conformance by \textbf{+7.7\,pp}
over test-only feedback (E6) on the same 120 tasks under a controlled
feedback-variant comparison.
We provide a \textbf{deployment boundary table} (C4): test-feedback B2 is the
default on aggregate conformance and unfiltered corpora (E10: B2 89.1\% vs.\ M
83.1\%; E12: B2 leads all seeds); full mode~M is warranted when conjunctive
$\mathrm{Accept}$ or prevention (PDR/FAR) is required, not from overlap
tertiles alone (canonical E3 is non-monotone).

On the current 120-task hard benchmark (E1 canonical, seed~0), B1 reaches
89.2\% mean conformance versus M at 86.3\%---single-repeat ranking is
seed-sensitive; E12-full (120 tasks $\times$ 3 seeds) shows B2 leads every seed
(M wins 4/120 on mean-across-seeds pairing; Friedman $p{=}0.077$; B2 ranks
first on all three seeds).
M vs.\ B2 on E1 canonical is not Holm-significant ($p{=}1.0$).
Conjunctive acceptance yields 5.0\% strict success on current BENCH-120 but lower escaped defects
(PDR 95.0\%, FAR 5.0\% vs.\ B2).
Tasks, logs, and scripts are released.

\paragraph{Keywords.}
Specification-guided repair; ordered guard specifications; LLM synthesis; SOFL;
counterexample-guided repair; SMT verification; deployment boundary.
