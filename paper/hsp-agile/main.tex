\begin{abstract}
LLM-assisted implementation is increasingly used in agile development, but requirements-to-code defects remain difficult to prevent when behavior is specified by ordered formal scenarios.
This paper studies a conservative acceptance problem for Agile-SOFL tasks: under a bounded interaction budget, decide whether a generated implementation is acceptable with respect to both complete scenario-level functional agreement and requirement-fault safety constraints.
We present \textbf{HSP-Agile}, a hybrid pipeline that integrates (i) candidate synthesis, (ii) formal conformance checking, (iii) counterexample-guided repair, and (iv) high-severity pattern screening.
We formalize strict acceptance as a conjunctive predicate and evaluate it on a benchmark containing base Agile-SOFL tasks and precedence-sensitive hard synthetic tasks, using one-shot, iterative test-feedback, and ablation baselines under aligned budgets.
Empirical analysis follows a two-layer prevention protocol (specification-confusion and implementation-screening mutants) and reports strict correctness, prevention, and efficiency metrics.
To avoid over-claiming, we report only completed reruns and keep unresolved numerical entries as \texttt{TBD}.
\end{abstract}
