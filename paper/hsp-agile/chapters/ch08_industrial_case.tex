\section{Industrial Decision Vignette}
\label{sec:disc:industrial-case}

Consider a telecom-style ordered alert table in
\texttt{benchmarks/industrial\_sofl.json}: overlapping severity guards where
``link down'' must preempt ``high latency'' when both predicates hold.
Under gpt-4o / Claude on the $n{=}31$ industrial-pattern corpus, B2 already
reaches 100\% mean Conf.\ while M sits at 95.5\%
(Table~\ref{tab:cross-model-industrial})---so \emph{mean Conf.\ alone}
selects B2.

Apply the C4 rule: escalate to~M only if the sprint release bar requires
$\mathrm{Accept}{=}1$ (every SpecIR witness plus clear critical pattern screen)
or a prevention target FAR${\le}5\%$ on mutant screening (E2).
If the team only needs high mean Conf.\ before merge, keep B2 and avoid the
strengthened-$K$ bundle.
Overlap density is \emph{not} the trigger: E3 tertiles are non-monotone on
BENCH-120, and this industrial slice already favours B2 on Conf.

This vignette is curated industrial-\emph{pattern} SOFL, not a production
vendor trace; quantitative production KPIs are out of scope until a live
Agile-SOFL pilot is instrumented.
