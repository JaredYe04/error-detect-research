\chapter{Conclusion and Future Work}
\label{ch:conclusion}

LLM-assisted development can accelerate Agile-SOFL workflows, but it also
introduces specification-conformance defects: code that looks right on everyday
inputs yet violates first-match guard order on thin boundary regions.
The question was whether those defects can be prevented by using the
specification itself as evidence, without full deductive verification.
The answer developed here is the \textbf{Specification-guided Defect Prevention
Framework} (\textsc{SgDP}) and its SOFL/FSF instantiation \textbf{HSP-Agile}.


%=============================================================================
\section{Summary}
\label{sec:conc:summary}
%=============================================================================

\paragraph{Problem.}
Test-based repair loops miss precedence-sensitive faults because they do not
know which input regions the specification treats as critical.

\paragraph{Insight.}
Encode first-match priority as SMT witnesses, turn each failure into a typed
Semantic Feedback IR record, and release a candidate only when every witness
matches and no high-severity pattern remains.

\paragraph{Evidence.}
On a 120-task hard benchmark, HSP-Agile reaches 90.4\% mean strict formal
conformance (+6.2\,pp over one-shot LLM, +2.4\,pp over test-feedback).
Mechanism studies show the gain grows with guard overlap (E3), that SMT
witnesses keep boundary coverage where random sampling fails (E4), and that the
Semantic Feedback IR alone contributes +7.7\,pp over test-only prompts (E6).
Adapter-level experiments on Mini-Z and Mini-StateMachine support the framework
claim beyond SOFL/FSF.
Theorem~\ref{thm:witness-soundness} and Theorem~\ref{thm:far-bound} bound the
witness and acceptance arguments; Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:structured-feedback}
captures the empirically supported repair claim.

\paragraph{Limitation.}
Acceptance under budget $K=3$ remains selective (25.0\% strict success): many
tasks improve conformance without clearing every residual witness or pattern
hit.
E9 shows that ordering-repair and boundary/arithmetic failures dominate the
remainder.
Latency is the main cost (about $4.6\times$ vs.\ test-feedback).


%=============================================================================
\section{Key Empirical Findings}
\label{sec:conc:findings}
%=============================================================================

Five results summarise the evidence.
Conformance, not binary pass/fail, is the right primary metric: M leads on
mean $\mathrm{Conf}$ while its slightly lower strict-success rate reflects
the intentionally stricter conjunctive gate
(Section~\ref{sec:disc:paradox})---the same selectivity that rejects a
hard-coded \code{classify\_signal} even when most witnesses pass.
Repair is the largest ablation drop (A3: $-6.3$\,pp; A1: $-3.9$\,pp).
Structure in feedback matters more than prompt length: E6's full Semantic
Feedback IR outperforms test-only and test-plus-expected variants by more
than 7\,pp.
Harder specifications amplify the method: the M--B1 gap is largest in the
dense-overlap tertile (E3).
Finally, the loop transfers across notations: Mini-Z and Mini-StateMachine
improve with adapter-only changes (E8).


%=============================================================================
\section{Future Research Directions}
\label{sec:conc:future}
%=============================================================================

\paragraph{Richer specification support.}
Extend beyond linear integer guards to temporal properties, stateful behaviours,
and non-linear arithmetic---directly targeting the ArithmeticError and
StateError residues in E9.

\paragraph{Stronger backends.}
Augment the finite witness suite with theorem-prover certificates
(e.g.\ Dafny~\citep{leino2010dafny}) for candidates that already clear
$\mathrm{Accept}$.

\paragraph{Adaptive budgets.}
Replace fixed $K=3$ with early stopping on easy tasks and extra iterations on
dense-overlap ordering failures.

\paragraph{Industrial replication.}
Apply HSP-Agile to practitioner SOFL/FSF specifications and measure prevention
against project acceptance criteria.

\paragraph{Model comparison.}
Re-run the 120-task matrix across stronger instruction-following models to test
whether the +7.7\,pp IR effect is model-agnostic.


%=============================================================================
\section{Closing Remarks}
\label{sec:conc:closing}
%=============================================================================

\textsc{SgDP} is not a substitute for full formal verification.
It is a practical bridge: bounded formal checking, scenario-aware repair, and
conjunctive acceptance that keep many specification-conformance defects---the
ordering failures illustrated by \code{classify\_signal}---out of release
while remaining usable inside a sprint.
How far that bridge extends depends on richer encodings, industrial tasks, and
adaptive repair---the directions above.
