Given an FSF specification, HSP-Agile parses \textbf{SpecIR} regions
$R_i = G_i \wedge \bigwedge_{j<i}\neg G_j$, synthesises an LLM candidate, and
iterates repair up to $K{=}3$ attempts.
Failed attempts emit a typed \textbf{Semantic Feedback IR}: scenario label,
violated guard, SMT witness input, expected postcondition, and optional
pattern-guard hits---rendered into the repair prompt without free-form
verbal memory.

Release is conjunctive. With $\tau{=}1$ and high-severity pattern count
$P^H(c)$:
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{Accept}(c,t)
  \;\iff\;
  \mathrm{Conf}(c,t)\geq\tau
  \;\wedge\;
  P^H(c)=0.
  \label{eq:accept-conf}
\end{equation}
Mode~M applies hard pattern screening before Accept; B2 uses unstructured
test pass/fail only.
Ablations A1/A3 remove the formal checker or the repair loop (A2 removes the
pattern guard; $\Delta\mathrm{Conf}$ reported in Results).

The Semantic Feedback IR is the repair hinge: under E6 ($n{=}120$, $K{=}3$),
full IR (variant~C) reaches 86.9\% mean Conf.\ vs.\ 79.1\% for test-only
variant~A---isolating specification structure from prompt volume under the
unstructured-test baseline (E14 scopes the claim against length-matched
traces; see Results).
